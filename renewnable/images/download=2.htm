    How often do solar thermal panels need to be serviced and maintained?

    Solar thermal panels don’t need servicing that often, but it’s wise to keep them well-maintained by checking on them two or three times a year. In general, it’s recommended that you get them serviced annually, if convenient.

    The best way to maintain solar thermals is to clean them fairly regularly, as dirt and grime can badly affect their ability to absorb sunlight.

    You can rely on professionals to provide your solar thermal systems with the servicing and maintenance they need.

    Does solar thermal work in the winter?

    Yes, solar thermal systems work perfectly fine even in the coldest of winters. After all, they aren’t affected by temperature, just the amount of sunlight they are able to gather. Of course, winter brings in shorter days, darker mornings and evenings, as well as cloudier weather all round, which can negatively affect the overall energy output of your solar thermal systems, as they aren’t able to absorb as much sunlight compared to the spring or summer months.

    Is solar thermal worth it in the UK?

    Solar thermal systems are definitely worth the investment if you’re in any way dissatisfied with your current water heaters. East Anglia is one of the sunniest regions in the UK, which makes it a positively delightful home for any sun-starved solar thermal system. With solar thermals, you’ll be able to enjoy consistently hot water temperatures all year round while saving greatly on your energy bills.

    Furthermore, solar thermal panels are more environmentally friendly due to their sustainable power source. When used to heat water, solar radiation carries next to no drawbacks – there are no harmful byproducts involved and, in effect, it’s infinitely renewable, so you’ll never run out of power for your solar thermal system.

    How do solar thermal systems work?

    There are two main types of solar thermals to consider.

    • Solar thermal panels
    • Evacuated solar thermal tubes

    They both work in roughly the same way, but absorb solar energy using differently shaped collection surfaces – one being flat, and the other having layers of round, emptied tubes.

    Both the thermal panels and evacuated tubes gather energy from the sun’s radiation via thousands of photovoltaic solar cells, which are designed to convert this energy into usable electricity.

    Solar thermal cells channel electrical energy into water heating, working much the same way as a traditional electrical heating system.

    Where can solar thermal panels be installed?

    Like solar PV panels, solar thermal panels are most ideally situated in an elevated, southerly-facing position to gather the most sunlight. However, we understand that this is not always feasible, either due to the rooftops being incompatible or an overall lack of available space. To accommodate customers with spacing issues, we can compromise with ground-level solar thermal support beams.
